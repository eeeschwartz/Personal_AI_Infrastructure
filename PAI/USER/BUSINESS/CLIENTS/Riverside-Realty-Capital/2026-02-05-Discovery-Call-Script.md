## PHASE 2: OPEN DISCOVERY (3-12 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand THEIR specific bottleneck. Don't assume—branch based on what they said.

---

### BRANCH A: If He Mentioned DEAL FLOW / FINDING DEALS

**YOU:** "Tell me more about that. How are you currently sourcing deals? Walk me through 'new opportunity' to 'we're interested.'"

**Listen for:**
- Broker relationships vs. platforms vs. direct outreach
- Deal volume (how many per month? How many make it past screening?)
- How they track opportunities (spreadsheet? CRM? email?)
- Who screens deals initially?

**Probe deeper:**
- "How much time are you spending searching for deals vs. actually closing them?"
- "Are you finding enough deals, or is quality the issue?"
- "Where are your best deals coming from? Can that be scaled?"

**What you're looking for:**
- Manual market scanning (could be automated with AI agents)
- Relationship management challenges (CRM/tracking)
- Time spent on low-quality leads

---

### BRANCH B: If He Mentioned OPERATIONS / DUE DILIGENCE / PROCESSING DEALS

**YOU:** "Walk me through what happens once you've found a deal you're interested in. What's the process?"

**Listen for:**
- Lease abstraction (manual or automated?)
- Financial analysis (T-12, rent rolls—manual data entry?)
- Document tracking (how do they manage deadlines and docs?)
- Timeline (how long does DD typically take?)
- Team size (is it just Lev? Small team?)

**Probe deeper on specific areas:**

**Lease abstraction:**
"When you get a 50-unit multifamily package with 50 leases—how do you process those leases? Who extracts the key terms?"

**Financial analysis:**
"Are you manually entering data from T-12s and rent rolls into your models, or is that automated?"

**Deal tracking:**
"How do you track where each deal is in the process? Spreadsheet? What happens when someone asks 'Where are we on the XYZ deal?'"

**What you're looking for:**
- Manual lease abstraction (3-5 hrs/lease = huge bottleneck)
- Manual data entry from financial docs
- Scattered tracking (spreadsheets, email, folders)
- No standardized workflow

---

### BRANCH C: If He Mentioned SCALING / GROWTH / CAN'T GROW FAST ENOUGH

**YOU:** "What's limiting your ability to scale? Is it deal flow, capital, bandwidth, or something else?"

**Listen for what the constraint is:**

**If DEAL FLOW:**
→ "How are you finding deals now? Is volume the issue or quality?"

**If CAPITAL:**
→ "Are you spending a lot of time on fundraising, LP management, investor relations?"

**If BANDWIDTH:**
→ "What's taking up most of your time right now? Where are you the bottleneck?"

**Probe deeper:**
- "If you had unlimited [constraint], how many deals could you close per year?"
- "What breaks first when you try to scale? Team? Process? Systems?"

**What you're looking for:**
- Process bottlenecks preventing scale
- Manual tasks eating up time
- No systematic approach (every deal is bespoke)

---

### BRANCH D: If He Mentioned COMPETITION / "COMPETITORS USING AI"

**YOU:** "What are you seeing competitors do with AI that you're not? What's catching your attention?"

**Listen for:**
- Specific tools or approaches they've heard about
- Competitive disadvantage they're feeling
- FOMO vs. actual strategic need

**Probe deeper:**
- "Have you looked at any tools or talked to any vendors?"
- "What held you back from implementing something already?"
- "What would success look like if you implemented AI?"

**What you're looking for:**
- Real competitive threat vs. just curiosity
- Budget availability (if they've explored, they have budget)
- Decision-making process (solo or team decision?)

---

### BRANCH E: If He Mentioned TEAM / PEOPLE / HIRING

**YOU:** "What's the challenge with the team? Is it hiring, retention, training, or something else?"

**Listen for:**
- What roles are hardest to fill
- What tasks Lev is doing that he wishes he could delegate
- Whether automation could replace headcount needs

**Probe deeper:**
- "What roles are you trying to hire for right now?"
- "If you could clone yourself, what would you have that clone do?"
- "Are there tasks you're doing manually that could be automated instead of hiring for?"

**What you're looking for:**
- Tasks that could be automated instead of hiring
- Delegation opportunities
- Process standardization needs before hiring

---

### BRANCH F: If He Mentioned LP REPORTING / INVESTOR RELATIONS

**YOU:** "Tell me about your LP reporting process. How often do you report? What's involved?"

**Listen for:**
- Frequency (monthly? quarterly?)
- Manual data collection across properties
- Time spent compiling reports
- Standardization of reporting

**Probe deeper:**
- "How much time goes into preparing those reports each month/quarter?"
- "Are you pulling data manually from multiple sources?"
- "Do your LPs ask for custom reports or ad-hoc analysis?"

**What you're looking for:**
- Manual report compilation
- Data scattered across systems
- Custom/ad-hoc requests eating time

### UNIVERSAL FOLLOW-UPS (Use for any branch)

After they've explained their process:

## PHASE 3: PROBLEM EXTRACTION (12-20 minutes)

**Goal:** Get crystal clear on the root problem and its cost.

### Deep Dive Questions (Adapt based on their bottleneck)

**For ANY bottleneck, ask these:**

1. **Current State:** "Walk me through the last time this happened. What did it look like?"

2. **Failure Point:** "What specifically breaks down? Where does it go wrong?"

3. **Cost/Impact:** "What's this costing you? Time? Money? Deals? Relationships?"

4. **Ideal State:** "If this was working perfectly, what would that look like? Paint me a picture."

5. **Attempts:** "What have you tried to fix this? Why didn't it work?"

### Synthesis

**YOU:** "Okay, so here's what I'm hearing:

**Current state:** [Summarize where they are now]
**The problem:** [Summarize the bottleneck]
**The cost:** [Quantify the impact]
**Ideal state:** [Describe their vision]

Does that capture it?"

**[Get confirmation]**

### STRATEGIC INSIGHTS (Choose based on what you discovered)

#### If LEASE ABSTRACTION is the bottleneck:

**YOU:** "Here's what I'm seeing, Lev. The reason lease abstraction is killing you is because you're treating it like a reading comprehension problem. Most people hire someone to read leases and extract terms. But it's actually a **pattern recognition problem**—AI can extract 200+ standard lease fields in 7 minutes with 99% accuracy.

The tools exist right now—Cactus, Prophia, others—but most firms either don't know they exist or don't know how to integrate them into their workflow. **That's the gap.**

It's not about the tech. It's about mapping your workflow FIRST, then plugging in the right automation at the right place."

---

#### If FINANCIAL ANALYSIS / DATA ENTRY is the bottleneck:

**YOU:** "The issue with T-12 and rent roll analysis isn't the math—it's the **data entry**. You're probably spending 80% of your time manually copying numbers from PDFs into Excel, and 20% actually analyzing them.

The leverage point is automating that data extraction so you can spend 95% of your time on analysis and underwriting, not copy-paste. There are AI tools that can parse those documents in seconds now with 99% accuracy, but you need the right workflow around them.

Process FIRST, automation SECOND."

---

#### If DEAL TRACKING / SCATTERED SYSTEMS is the bottleneck:

**YOU:** "The problem with tracking deals across spreadsheets and email is you're **building a new system for every deal**. No institutional memory. No standardization.

When I work with acquisition firms, I start by mapping your actual workflow—what happens at each stage, who's responsible, what documents are needed—and then we build automation around that map.

The goal isn't to buy software; it's to **standardize your process FIRST**, then automate the repeatable parts. Most firms skip step one and end up with expensive tools that don't fit their workflow."

---

#### If OPERATOR EVALUATION is the bottleneck:

**YOU:** "The challenge with operator evaluation is it's usually **subjective and inconsistent**. One person likes an operator, another doesn't. There's no institutional knowledge.

What you need is a standardized framework—track record metrics, financial capability, asset management approach—that turns it into an objective scorecard. Then you can build automation around the data collection.

The key is: **Process FIRST, automation SECOND**. Define what 'good operator' means, THEN automate the evaluation."

---

#### If DEAL FLOW / SOURCING is the bottleneck:

**YOU:** "The challenge with deal sourcing in secondary and tertiary markets is there's **no centralized data**. Bigger markets have platforms and data providers. Your markets? You're manually scanning.

The leverage point is using AI agents to continuously monitor markets based on your criteria—property type, price range, location, cap rate—and alert you when something matches. Think of it as a 24/7 scout that never sleeps.

But here's the key: You need to define your criteria FIRST. **Process mapping** before automation. Otherwise you're automating garbage in, garbage out."

---

#### If SCALING / BANDWIDTH is the bottleneck:

**YOU:** "The reason you can't scale isn't that you don't have the capital or the deals—it's that your **process doesn't scale**. Every deal is bespoke. Every workflow is manual.

The firms that scale effectively do one thing: They **standardize their process**, then automate the repeatable parts. They turn 'how we do deals' from tribal knowledge into a system.

That's what process mapping does. We document your workflow, find the manual bottlenecks, then systematically eliminate them. THEN you can scale."

---

#### If LP REPORTING / INVESTOR RELATIONS is the bottleneck:

**YOU:** "The problem with LP reporting isn't that it's hard—it's that it's **manual data collection from 10 different sources**. By the time you've pulled the data, formatted it, and sent it out, half your week is gone.

The leverage point is centralizing your portfolio data so reporting becomes a button click, not a multi-day project. AI can pull data from operator reports, financial statements, property management systems—whatever you've got—and auto-populate your LP reports.

But you need to **standardize the format first**. Process before automation."

---

#### If COMPETITIVE PRESSURE is the driver:

**YOU:** "Here's the thing about AI in commercial real estate: Most firms are **playing with toys**, not building systems. They buy a chatbot or a lease abstraction tool without mapping their process first.

You have an advantage because you're thinking about this strategically. The question isn't 'What AI tool should I buy?' It's '**Where are my bottlenecks, and what's the ROI of fixing them?**'

That's what process mapping does. We find the leverage points, THEN we talk tech. Otherwise you're guessing."

---

### Position Your Approach (2 minutes)

**YOU:** "Here's how I typically approach this, and it's based on a methodology called **'Automate to Accelerate.'**

**Step 1:** We map your current process from end to end. Every step, every handoff, every document.

**Step 2:** We identify what I call 'automation opportunities'—the repetitive, high-volume tasks that are bottlenecking you.

**Step 3:** We pick ONE quick win—usually something that can show ROI in 30-60 days—and build a proof of concept.

**Step 4:** Once that's working, we expand to the rest of your workflow.

**The reason this works** is because most firms try to automate everything at once and fail. We go **process-first, then automation, then scale**."

### Optional: Quick Example (1 minute, only if time permits)

**YOU:** "For example, I worked with a firm that was spending 15 hours per deal on lease abstraction. We mapped their workflow, identified the bottleneck, and got them down to 2 hours using AI extraction tools.

The key wasn't the tech—it was mapping the RIGHT process first."
