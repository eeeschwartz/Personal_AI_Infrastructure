## PHASE 2: DISCOVER THE BOTTLENECK (3-12 min)

**⚠️ CRITICAL: Branch based on what HE said, don't assume acquisition workflow**

### If He Mentioned OPERATIONS/WORKFLOWS:
"Tell me more about that. Walk me through what that looks like day-to-day."

**THEN dive into workflow questions:**

**Deal Sourcing:** "How do you source deals? Walk me through 'new opportunity' to 'we're interested.'"
- Listen for: Volume, tracking, who screens

**Due Diligence:** "Walk me through DD. What are the key steps?"
- **PROBE:** "When you get a 50-unit package with 50 leases, T-12s, rent rolls—how do you process that? Who extracts lease terms?"
- Listen for: Lease abstraction (3-5 hrs/lease?), manual data entry, timeline

**Operator Evaluation:** "How do you vet operators? What's that process?"
- Listen for: Standardization, relationship tracking

**Portfolio Monitoring:** "How do you monitor performance vs. underwriting after closing?"
- Listen for: Manual reporting, spreadsheets, visibility

---

### If He Mentioned SCALING/GROWTH:
"What's limiting your ability to scale? Is it deal flow, capital, bandwidth, or something else?"

**THEN explore the constraint:**
- Deal flow constraint → "How do you currently find deals? What's working/not working?"
- Capital constraint → "Are you spending time on LP management, fundraising?"
- Bandwidth constraint → "What's taking up most of your time right now?"

---

### If He Mentioned COMPETITIVE PRESSURE:
"What are competitors doing with AI that you're not?"

**THEN explore the gap:**
- "Have you looked at any tools or approached any vendors?"
- "What held you back from implementing?"

---

### If He Mentioned TEAM/PEOPLE:
"What's the challenge? Hiring, retention, training, or something else?"

**THEN explore talent bottleneck:**

### If He's VAGUE (Most Likely):

## PHASE 4: STRATEGIC INSIGHT (20-25 min)

### Pick ONE Insight Based on What You Heard:

**If LEASE ABSTRACTION:**
"Here's what I'm seeing. You're treating lease abstraction like a reading problem. It's actually pattern recognition—AI extracts 200+ fields in 7 minutes with 99% accuracy. Tools exist (Cactus, Prophia) but most firms don't know how to integrate them into workflow. That's the gap."

**If FINANCIAL ANALYSIS:**
"The issue isn't the math—it's data entry. You're spending 80% on copy-paste, 20% analyzing. The leverage is automating extraction so you spend 95% on analysis. Tools exist, but you need the right workflow."

**If DEAL TRACKING:**
"The problem is you're building a new system for every deal. No institutional memory. I start by mapping your workflow—what happens at each stage, who's responsible, what docs needed—then automate the repeatable parts. Process FIRST, software SECOND."

**If OPERATOR MGMT:**
"Operator evaluation is usually subjective. You need a standardized scorecard—track record metrics, financial capability, asset mgmt approach. Then automate the data collection. Process FIRST, automation SECOND."

### Position Your Approach (2 min)

"Here's my methodology—'Automate to Accelerate':

1. **Map** your process end-to-end
2. **Identify** automation opportunities (repetitive, high-volume bottlenecks)
3. **Pick ONE quick win** (ROI in 30-60 days)
4. **Expand** once that's working

Most firms try to automate everything at once and fail. We go process-first, then automation, then scale."

## 🔥 IF THINGS GO SIDEWAYS

**"We're doing fine with current process"**
→ "Great! How many deals per quarter? Limited by flow, capital, or bandwidth? If unlimited bandwidth, how many could you close? [Listen] That gap is where automation creates leverage."

**"We tried AI tools—didn't work"**
→ "What'd you try? [Listen] Yeah, super common. Most plug in tools without mapping process first. Like buying a drill before knowing where the holes go. That's why I start with mapping—WHAT to automate before HOW."

**"I'm not technical"**
→ "Perfect—neither are most clients. That's ideal because you won't over-engineer it. Best automations are simple. My job is translating your process without you becoming a tech expert."

**"Just tell me what software to buy"**
→ "I could, but you'd waste money. Every firm's workflow is different. Let me map yours first, THEN I tell you exactly what to use and how to configure it. Otherwise you're guessing."
