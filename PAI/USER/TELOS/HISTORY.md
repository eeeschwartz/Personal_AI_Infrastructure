# History

## Early Life
- Grew up in Albion, MI. Parents were from the East Coast and randomly ended up in this small town in Michigan. Always had a sense of being an outsider. Dad in particular was from New Jersey and imparted a chip on your shoulder attitude.

## Music Years
- Began playing outsider rock music in high school. It was huge. Met friend Sam Hunt who was great at digging into the underground art and music scene. Spent high school listening to records in his garage and writing music together. Became a very good drummer.
- Went to Oberlin College where I encountered a near majority of kids who were hip and plugged into the underground. Many from the coasts with a closer claim to the source of the trends. It threw me off and rather than compete, I dropped off, stopped making music seriously.
- Despite withdrawal, formed a short-lived but promising band during sophomore year. Older bandmate invited me to play music and live in his art collective in the heart of the Chicago scene. It was huge. Played, wrote, and toured for 5 years achieving a taste of success.

## Career Transition
- During this period met wife Smith. Married and life changed. Dove into life-long interest in technology, became a professional software engineer at a small web development company.
- Forged strong friendships and grew the company from 7 people to 40 in 4 years, becoming a partner.
- Enjoyed the software engineering craft but the business side didn't lock in at that time. Used relationship capital to ask to go remote. Became an early version of a digital nomad with Smith traveling around the western hemisphere for a few years.

## Purpose Seeking
- Similar to music career it was fun but ultimately unfulfilling. Sought purpose and landed a yearlong fellowship in the peace corps for geeks, aka Code for America. Exhilarating to be in San Francisco working with teammates who had rescued the massive healthcare.gov rollout from a total dumpster fire.

## Family & Lexington
- After the year Smith and I got pregnant and moved to her hometown of Lexington, KY to be near family. Had first daughter Elliot and continued work as a civic consultant within the city government of Lexington for a couple of years.
- Escape hatch from the bureaucracy was to join hands with Drura, a cult-like figure in his VC-backed manufacturing startup called MakeTime. Hated it. Super unclear what we were doing together in that office.
- Around this time had second daughter Polly. Felt an unbelievable lack of autonomy crushing my day to day.

## Entrepreneurship
- Forced to be disciplined. Started a land flipping side hustle. First solo business. Got up at 5am to post desert properties on Craigslist for $100/month over 36 months. It worked well enough. Leveled up and eventually sold a few properties for tens of thousands in profit. Gave a lot of confidence.
- Drura pulled an absolutely unbelievable rabbit out of a hat as runway was rapidly depleting. Sold MakeTime to a competitor as an all-stock acquihire. Luck for us this company, Xometry, was able to go public a year later and we all made a decent chunk of change. A few years of salary type of thing.
- That bureaucracy got old as the company grew from 100 to 800 and several of us bailed to form a new startup, BCOM.
- That was me, Drura, Ryan and later Tom James. Good team. Swung for the fences as a bootstrapped company. No traction. Invested 15 months of time and $40k. All went to zero. Drura and I scorched our relationship on the way out.

## Reset & Renewal
- Redoubled role as a dad in the first months without the startup intensity. Spent the summer taking girls to the pool and taking vacations. Was awesome.
- After several months wanted a path back to making money. Returned to land flipping. Didn't love it, wasn't successful, probably in the reverse order. Decided to market software to the land flipping community rather than actually flipping.
- Recruited a co-founder, incorporated, and tried to market and sell-sell-sell. Something I don't do naturally. After a lot of pushing and no traction, hung it up.

## Front Row Dads & AI
- In July 2024 found way to a community called Front Row Dads. A few hundred amazing men, most of which have had tremendous business success and seen it break their families. Now they come together several times a year in the spirit of personal, family, and community development. A tremendously vulnerable and loving group who showed up just when needed.
- A lawyer friend named Brad tapped me just then to polish his AI prototype focused on streamlining document prep for defense lawyers. Thought it could be a really strong company. Returned to software dev after a couple years off, which was fun with AI at my back.
- But he had another legal business that was very successful and time consuming and he didn't do his side of the legal startup which was to sell. That was enough to convince me I needed to sell my own work.

## Current (2025+)
- It was 2025 and I was well positioned to help businesses adopt AI to break through bottlenecks.
- Went all in and got first major client in September of 2025. They pay a full time retainer for 5-10 hours of work a week. Yatzee. They're great to work with and I love the puzzle of creating value for them.
