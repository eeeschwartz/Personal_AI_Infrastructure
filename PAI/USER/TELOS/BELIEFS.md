# Convictions

**Core beliefs that guide decisions and worldview.**

---

## CV1: Personal Connection

We need vastly more personal connection.

---

## CV2: Community Narratives

We need narratives that encourage community connection and agency. This will spread from the bottom up to improve faith in systems.

**See:** Bowling Alone research

---

## CV3: Wealth vs Community

Our narratives over-index on individualistic wealth builders like Hormozi, not community builders. When a brilliant wealth builder pits against a brilliant community builder, wealth always wins.

---

## CV4: Convictions Loosely Held

We must develop deep convictions, loosely held to give ourselves room to explore truth in an impossibly complex world. It requires courage.

**h/t:** Ryan Levesque
**Addresses:** C2 (Deep Convictions challenge)

---

## CV5: AI Economic Disruption

AI is going to gut the economy as we know it. We must find meaning outside of wealth and career. Our best hope is to use the current abundance to build resilient local networks. It's a win-win. Maybe we don't collapse. This threads the needle between what we desperately need now existentially and what we may desperately need later economically and ecologically.

**Addresses:** P1, P2, P3

---

## CV6: Money vs Relationships

In today's status hierarchy, people overvalue money and undervalue relationships.

---

## CV7: Ecological Risk

Risk is **ecological**, not individual: when community erodes, no personal "walled garden" is enough.

---

## CV8: Haves and Have-Nots

It is very likely that ecological and economic collapse will manifest as "haves" who live in walled gardens with abundance and "have-nots" who live in squalor barely surviving.

---

## CV9: People-First Community Building

Find the people first — everything else (land, structure, homestead, shared economy) figures itself out from there. A community built around the right 1-3 families with shared values and complementary talents will outperform any carefully planned intentional community that starts with a place. Talent and alignment are the scarce resource, not land.

**Source:** ReTribalize community (@ReTribalize)
**Addresses:** G4, C1

---

## CV10: Simple Living as Appreciation Practice

Living simply — as a craftsman, outdoors, close to nature — is not asceticism. It is a practice for noticing and appreciating what you already have. Inspired by minimalism, the Comfort Crisis (comfort box avoidance), and the belief that modern consumption numbs rather than fulfills. You don't need less to be happy; you need *friction* to recognize what matters.

**Source:** Comfort Crisis (Michael Easter), minimalism tradition
**Addresses:** P2

---

## CV11: Family-First Entrepreneurship

Your children need a present father more than a wealthy one. Business serves family — not the other way around. The Front Row Dads model: be a world-class dad who also builds meaningful work, rather than a world-class businessman who tries to also be a dad.

**Source:** Front Row Dads community (deeply embedded member)
**Addresses:** C2 (building convictions worth holding)

---

## CV12: The AI/Simplification Paradox

AI is an energy-intensive tool that may itself be simplified away as Nate Hagens' Great Simplification reduces the energy baseline underpinning the economy. We are in a brief strategic window of extraordinary AI capability and pre-simplification abundance. Use this window now — intentionally — to build human systems (community, skills, relationships, local resilience) that are durable with or without AI. Those who use this window only for wealth accumulation will be the most fragile when it closes. Those who use it to build human infrastructure will be the most resilient.

**Source:** Nate Hagens (Great Simplification), Ryan Levesque (Digital Contrarian)
**Addresses:** P1, P2, P3, CV5

---

## CV13: Somatic State Drives Outcomes (80/20)

Somatic state — the body's nervous system, breath, presence, physical regulation — accounts for roughly 80% of your capacity to achieve goals. Mindset accounts for only 20%. Most self-improvement culture inverts this, obsessing over mindset while ignoring the body. Transformation requires working from the body up, not the mind down.

**Source:** The Bridge Method, Extreme Leadership workshops (Kate's transformation), Preston Smiles somatic work
**Addresses:** P2, C2

---

## CV14: Somatic Experience Will Grow in Inverse Proportion to AI

As AI expands and colonizes the cognitive domain, embodied presence — somatic experience, physical skill, being fully in the body — becomes increasingly rare, valuable, and distinctly human. Interest in somatic health, craftsman work, nature immersion, and physical practice will grow in direct proportion to AI expansion. The body is the last irreducibly human domain.

**Source:** Preston Smiles, observation of AI trajectory
**Connects:** CV12 (AI/Simplification Paradox), CV10 (Simple Living / Craftsman)

---

## CV15: Backbone is Revealed, Not Built

Your strength, purpose, and masculine power are already present. The work is not to accumulate more — it is to carve away the conditioning, numbing, and protective masks that hide what was always there. Self-development is subtractive, not additive.

**Source:** David Wagner, *Backbone*
**Connects:** CV4 (convictions loosely held — you already have convictions, uncover them), C2 (changing direction under setbacks — the pivot is a mask, not the truth)
