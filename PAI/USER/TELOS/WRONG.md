# Things I've Been Wrong About

**Intellectual humility is a superpower.**

---

## Past Beliefs That Were Wrong

### I don't need to actively engage in goal setting
- **What I believed:** I could improvise my way through life
- **Reality:** Without goals, I drift and pivot endlessly (see C2)
- **Lesson:** Structure enables freedom

### I don't need to cultivate discipline. I'm an improviser. I'm a jazz player.
- **What I believed:** My creative, improvisational nature was sufficient
- **Reality:** Discipline is what allowed the land flipping side hustle to work (5am Craigslist posts)
- **Lesson:** Jazz players practice scales religiously

### I don't need to actively pursue mentorship
- **What I believed:** I could figure it out myself
- **Reality:** Front Row Dads showed up exactly when needed
- **Lesson:** Community accelerates growth

### Saving money is more important than developing myself
- **What I believed:** Financial security first
- **Reality:** Self-development creates more value than hoarding
- **Lesson:** Invest in yourself

### I thought Austin would be a disaster by now due to water shortages
- **What I believed:** Austin's water situation would cause major problems by now
- **Reality:** Still functioning fine
- **Lesson:** Timelines for systemic problems are hard to predict
