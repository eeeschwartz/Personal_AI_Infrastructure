## N3: Intentional Community as Response to Great Simplification

The instinct to "go build a farm and live off the land" is actually about community — the farm is incidental. The real goal is to be with aligned people, live simply, and build something durable. That's not dropping out of society — it's building the society we actually need, as a win-win: it addresses the current meaning crisis (we need belonging and purpose now) AND prepares us for the Great Simplification (resilient local networks survive energy contraction). Start with 1-3 families, iterate, don't romanticize the homestead before you have the people.

**Supports:** M2
**Addresses:** P2, P3
**Source:** ReTribalize, Nate Hagens, Front Row Dads

---

## N4: The Strategic Window

We are living in a brief period of extraordinary abundance: AI tools, cheap energy, global connectivity. Most people are using this window to optimize for wealth and status. A better use is to build durable human infrastructure — community, skills, relationships, local resilience — that would survive whether AI continues to scale or gets simplified away. The people who build human systems during this window will be the most resilient. This is not pessimism; it's strategic clarity about what actually lasts.

**Supports:** M1, M2
**Addresses:** P1, P2, P3
**Source:** Nate Hagens (Great Simplification), CV12
